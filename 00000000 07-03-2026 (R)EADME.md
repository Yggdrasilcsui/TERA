TERA for AI Systems
Overview
TERA (Temporal Entropy & Resource Alignment) is a meta-level algorithm designed to evaluate the stability and relevance of information in dynamic systems.
TERA does not replace domain models or physical laws.
Instead, it operates as a logical stability layer above data, models, and sensors, helping AI systems determine whether information remains coherent and decision-ready under changing conditions.
TERA evaluates information under three fundamental constraints:
Energy / resources
time evolution
contextual coupling
If information remains coherent under these constraints, it can be accepted for decision processes.
Core Idea
Most AI systems process data statically.
However, real-world information changes with time, context, and system dynamics.
TERA introduces a stability check that answers a simple question:
Is this information still valid within the current dynamic context?
The algorithm evaluates information across nested layers of relevance and stability.

Decision Gates
TERA reduces complex evaluation to three primary decision states:

ACCEPT  → Information is stable and usable
WAIT    → Information is incomplete or unstable
REJECT  → Information contradicts system constraints
WAIT acts as an explicit non-decision state, preventing premature conclusions when systems are in transition.
